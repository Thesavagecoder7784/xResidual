# Why each position — the theses

Four paper positions, each expressing a *different* documented finding. All are either
model-light (anchored to a liquid market) or structurally grounded, and all sit on the two
market layers where we actually found inefficiency: the **new, thin elimination/group
markets** and the **structural format quirks** the crowd underweights. The deep, liquid
markets (winner, continent) are coherent and efficient, so there are no bets there.

---

## #1 — France champion, YES @ 0.150, $100  ·  *cross-market value*
**Why.** The deep, liquid winner market (~$1.6B volume) prices France, co-favourite, at
**16.2%**. The brand-new "stage of elimination" market prices France's champion leg at
**15.0%** — the *only* champion leg trading *below* the liquid winner market (every other
team's elim-champion leg sits above it). So I'm buying the same outcome cheaper than the
market that prices it best.
**Edge:** cross-market relative value, anchored to the liquid market.
**Risk:** high variance. It's ultimately a directional bet that France *wins the World Cup*;
the 1.2pp discount is the only edge, so the payoff needs the outcome. A value-flavoured
punt, not a near-certainty. (Sized accordingly.)

## #2 — Germany champion, NO @ 0.895, $100  ·  *new-market coherence fade*
**Why.** The elimination market prices Germany's title at **10.5%**; the liquid winner
market says **5.3%** — roughly 2× rich. That market is new, thin, ~27% overround, and
systematically overprices deep runs (it prices **1.42 champions** across all teams; only 1
can win). Buying NO bets Germany is *not* champion — ~94.7% likely per the liquid market —
at a price (0.895) implying only 89.5%.
**Edge:** coherence / convergence (pre-registered as **P11**), anchored to the liquid market.
**Risk:** a low-probability tail (Germany actually wins → lose the stake); high-probability,
low-return. Realizes as the new market converges toward the liquid one, so it needn't be
held to resolution.

## #3 — Senegal to advance, YES @ 0.72, $100  ·  *group-of-death cushioning (#16)*
**Why.** Senegal is in Group I (France/Senegal/Norway/Iraq — a "group of death"), so the
crowd underprices its advancement at **72%** out of panic. But the format-aware model (with
the 48-team **best-third safety net**) says **81%**: a strong side that finishes even third
usually grabs a best-third spot. The "to advance" market is liquid and coherent (legs sum to
31.8/32), and this uses the *blended* (reliable) ratings — so the 9pp gap is credible.
**Edge:** structural format mispricing — the best-third cushioning the crowd underweights.
*The cleanest of the four* (named finding + good model + coherent market).
**Risk:** model risk on Senegal's strength + single-tournament variance, but the structural
reason is robust.

## #4 — Brazil to win Group C, NO @ 0.285, $100  ·  *group-level favourite-overbet (#19/#15)*
**Why.** The market is **72%** sure Brazil tops Group C; the blended model says **57%**,
because Morocco (a 2022 semifinalist) is a real threat for first. Buying NO bets Brazil does
*not* finish 1st — model 43% vs the price's implied 28.5%. It's the favourite-longshot
instinct one layer down (#19). Ironic kicker (#15): Brazil may even be *better off* finishing
2nd (easier bracket path), so fading "Brazil wins the group" is doubly sensible.
**Edge:** favourite-overbet at the group level + a genuinely competitive group.
**Risk:** group-win is high variance (3 round-robin games + goal-difference tiebreakers; one
result can swing it), so a correct edge still has noisy realization.
