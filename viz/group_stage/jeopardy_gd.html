The jeopardy didn't vanish. It moved to goal difference.

"There's no real jeopardy in the group stage. Win one game and you're probably going through."
— the recurring complaint about the 48-team format
It's half right. In our 40,000-sim group stage, the cut for the last Round-of-32 ticket lands on exactly 3 points — one win — 83% of the time. So winning a game really is the price of entry.

But that's where the drama starts, not ends. When almost everyone on the bubble has the same record, the tiebreak does the cutting — and the first tiebreak is goal difference.

Same record, same points — so the tiebreak does the cutting. Every goal in every game, including the ones that already look won or lost, feeds the margin between the knockouts and the airport.
Live, and it's already happening: Opta puts a third-placed team finishing on GD 0 at 94.8% to qualify, but on GD −1 at 84.2% — a single goal of difference is worth roughly ten points of qualification probability.
